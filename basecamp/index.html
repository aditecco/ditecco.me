Why Basecamp is right for me

  • Business, with values

    You don't pursue business for the sake of business or sell-out-and-get-rich schemes; you try to build products with strong roots and a long-term vision;

  • THE digital product company

    You have perhaps the deepest digital product culture out there. And your books & blog earned you the role of an authoritative and respected voice in the industry.

  • The courage of authenticity

    You don't rely on trends or try to accomodate the dominant culture; you follow your original ideas and deliver great products, not caring about the rest.

  • Quality over quantity

    In all these years 37Signals faced many changes, and ultimately reshaped its entire product line. You had the strenght of focusing only on what works and makes sense.

  • Work different, to make a difference

    Many companies don't give workers the freedom to work remotely. You do, and you actively promote remote work. Plus, you wrote Rework (which I read), a modern-day pamphlet that disrupted all the common knowledge about work, competition, and the workplace.

  • No bulls--t

    Other job offers have the most unrealistic lists of requirements: you make it clear from the beginning that only good work counts, not super-powers.

  • Design meets science

    Your design work is never the product of random choices; you have a scientific approach. Design as an art form can deliver better results if supported by research and data.

You may be wondering what all this writing about Basecamp has to do with my applying to this job position. Well, Basecamp as a company has all the qualities in which I believe as a professional (and as an individual).

Old-School values, unbiased opinions, originality, authenticity, rationality. These principles, and the desire to build great products, are the reasons why Basecamp is where I want to work.

Now let me explain why I think I can be a good fit.

Why I can be the right choice for Basecamp

Over the past 3 years I completely devoted myself to ideating, building an selling my own products. I'm not talking about digital products, but physical ones, which are sometimes even more challenging. Some succeeded, others failed. My current entrepreneurial endeavor is Geekville, a coworking space for digital and creative professionals. The previous one was also a coworking space, The Collective.

Building and managing a coworking space means going trough the complete range of product business: design (both in the physical and digital realm), marketing & sales, pricing, admin, client acquisition, client retention, customer care, and more. While I certainly have still a lot to learn, I think this kind of first-hand experience can be a valuable asset for Basecamp.

In addition to this, I have an extensive expertise in User Interface design & Logo Design.

I worked in agencies, but they're not what I'm looking for. I worked as a freelance contractor, on products that lived and died in a couple of years or never saw the light of day altogether.

What I want today is working on meaningful, useful, long-standing products, and help selling them effectively trough good design and strategy.
